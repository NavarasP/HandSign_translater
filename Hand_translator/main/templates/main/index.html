About ASL

American Sign Language (ASL) is a complete, natural language that serves as the predominant sign language of Deaf communities in the United States and most of Anglophone Canada.

ASL is a visual language. With signing, the brain processes linguistic information through the eyes. The shape, placement, and movement of the hands, as well as facial expressions and body movements, all play important parts in conveying information.

ASL is not a form of English. It has its own distinct grammatical structure, which must be learned over time through study and practice. ASL is used predominantly in the United States and in many parts of Canada.

ENHANCING ACCESSIBILITY: SIGN LANGUAGE TRANSLATION

Our sign language translation system is a vital component in modern society, ensuring inclusion and equal opportunities for those with disabilities. We leverage advanced deep learning and neural network technologies to create accurate and efficient translation models.

Using convolutional neural networks (CNNs) and recurrent neural networks (RNNs), our system translates sign language in real-time, considering factors such as accuracy, speed, and resource efficiency. We're also exploring innovative ways to integrate tactile input and audio information, making our tools accessible for those who are blind or deaf.
