About ASL

American Sign Language (ASL) is a complete, natural language that serves as the predominant sign language of Deaf communities in the United States and most of Anglophone Canada. ASL is a visual language. With signing, the brain processes linguistic information through the eyes. The shape, placement, and movement of the hands, as well as facial expressions and body movements, all play important parts in conveying information.
